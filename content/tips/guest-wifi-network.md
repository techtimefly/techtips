- Open your router's admin page — type the address on its label (often 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1) into a browser.
- Find the "Guest Network" or "Guest Wi-Fi" setting and switch it on.
- Give it its own name and a separate password — don't reuse your main Wi-Fi password.
- Put visitors and smart-home gadgets — cameras, speakers, plugs — on the guest network; keep your phones and computers on the main one.
- A cheap smart device with weak security can't reach your computers or files when it's walled off this way.
